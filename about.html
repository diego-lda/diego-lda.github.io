About Me

I am a PhD candidate at School of Economics of the University of Bristol. My research focuses on the transaction networks that emerge from the use of digital currencies, in particular Bitcoin.

My formal training is in economics, econometrics and finance. I also have working experience in the banking sector and have dedicated time to software development.

CRYPTO DATA

Throughout my PhD I have worked with a variety of crypto data. For example, parsing raw blockchain data from a Bitcoin node and analysing transaction datasets in .csv format.

ECONOMETRICS

Exposed to current research in areas including network econometrics and distributional analysis. I have also produced research that applies non-stationary time series.

COMMUNICATION SKILLS

I have worked as teaching assistant in a variety of courses of both undergraduate and postgraduate level. Delivering seminars to students about complex econometric concepts.

SOFTWARE

As well as coding my research (mostly in python) I have experience with a variety of other languages. From Solidity smart contracts to Javascript webgames, I have had a broad exposure.
